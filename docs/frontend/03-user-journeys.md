# User Journeys

## Overview

Before designing screens, routing or frontend components, we first define the journey of each primary user.

A User Journey describes the long-term path a person follows while using DeutschHub to achieve their goal.

Unlike User Flows, User Journeys do not describe pages, buttons or navigation.

Instead, they describe the user's progress from their initial motivation to their final outcome.

These journeys ensure that every feature inside DeutschHub exists to support a meaningful stage in a user's life.

---

# Journey 1 — Study Abroad Learner

## Goal

Successfully prepare for studying or working in Germany.

## Journey

Dream
↓
Research
↓
Planning
↓
Learning German
↓
Meeting Requirements
↓
Application
↓
Preparation
↓
Life in Germany

## Journey Description

The learner begins with a dream of studying or working in Germany.

They first explore possible pathways, then choose an appropriate plan, begin learning German, prepare the required documents and eventually move to Germany.

DeutschHub accompanies them throughout the entire journey.

# Journey Relationship

Each journey is different, but they often intersect.

For example:

Study Abroad Learner
→ Learn German
→ Study in Germany

Traveler
→ Explore Germany
→ Experiences

Germany Enthusiast
→ Explore Germany
→ Learn German
→ Experiences

Germany Resident
→ Experiences
→ Account

Casual Visitor
→ Explore Germany
→ Learn German

---

# Design Principles

DeutschHub is designed around user journeys instead of isolated features.

Every feature should help users move forward in their personal journey.

The platform is successful when users always know:

- Where they are.
- What they have achieved.
- What they should do next.

Every business area contributes to at least one stage of a user's journey.
